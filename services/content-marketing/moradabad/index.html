Moradabad, Uttar Pradesh's brass handicraft capital positioned 165 km east of Delhi, hosts 890,000+ urban population generating ₹18,000+ crore economy anchored by brass-metalware manufacturing industry with 6,000+ export units producing 90% India's brass handicraft exports (₹6,500+ crore annual export turnover, USA-Europe-Middle East markets), electroplating-metal finishing services with 1,200+ units generating ₹1,400+ crore supporting jewelry-hardware industries, and agricultural trading hub contributing ₹2,800+ crore serving western Uttar Pradesh grain-sugarcane commerce. This export-dominated positioning creates international B2B content targeting overseas buyers-importers-retail chains, trade fair marketing content showcasing Frankfurt-Dubai-New York exhibition presence, and supply chain documentation content addressing logistics-customs-quality certification requirements for global handicraft trade.

Brass handicraft content emphasizes 400-year Mughal legacy (17th century artisan settlement) creating specialized metalworking expertise transmitted through family businesses. Export content demonstrates USA-Europe market dominance (60% Moradabad exports) serving home décor retail chains (Target-Walmart-HomeGoods), hospitality procurement (hotel-restaurant brass fixtures), and ethnic handicraft boutiques. Quality certification content showcases ISO compliance, lead-free brass standards, and DGFT export licensing building international buyer confidence. Delhi proximity content positions overnight trucking to IGI Airport cargo terminal and Mundra-JNPT ports enabling competitive logistics costs versus distant manufacturing locations.
