Case Study: Precision Forgings Manufacturer Achieves 280% GeM Order Growth Through Technical Content Strategy

Client: Tiruchirappalli-based forgings manufacturer supplying steam turbine components to BHEL and aftermarket customers, annual turnover ₹18 crore, struggling with GeM platform visibility despite 35-year manufacturing legacy and ISO 9001-14001 dual certification.

Challenge: GeM (Government e-Marketplace) digitization mandate requiring PSU suppliers register on centralized procurement platform created visibility crisis for established manufacturers accustomed to relationship-based orders. Technical documentation inadequacy where engineering drawings and metallurgical specifications presented in format optimized for BHEL quality teams proved incomprehensible to GeM procurement officers lacking engineering background. Competition from national suppliers offering generic components at lower prices without Tiruchirappalli manufacturing cluster specialized turbine component expertise.

Content Strategy Implementation (8-month campaign):
1. GeM Platform Optimization Content (Months 1-2): Created comprehensive GeM registration documentation including step-by-step video tutorials in Tamil-English demonstrating product catalog upload process, HSN code selection methodology for specialized forgings, and technical specification translation from BHEL drawing standards to GeM procurement terminology. Developed 24-page capability brochure converting technical manufacturing processes (open die forging, CNC machining, ultrasonic testing) into buyer-friendly content emphasizing delivery reliability and BHEL-validated quality systems, supported by factory floor photographic documentation and customer testimonial videos from maintenance engineers.
2. Technical Blogging for Procurement Decision-Maker Education (Months 3-5): Published 18 technical articles on company website addressing common turbine component maintenance challenges: "Steam Turbine Blade Failure Analysis: Material Selection vs. Operational Stresses," "Forging vs. Casting for Critical Power Plant Components: Lifecycle Cost Comparison," "BHEL Turbine Retrofit Parts Sourcing: OEM vs. Aftermarket Quality Standards." Content targeted maintenance managers and procurement engineers searching for component replacement solutions, positioning manufacturer as technical authority while naturally incorporating product offerings with BHEL part number cross-references.
3. LinkedIn B2B Networking Content (Months 3-8): Company director posted weekly updates documenting manufacturing processes, quality testing procedures, and customer delivery milestones, building personal brand authority within power plant engineering community. Shared industry insights on thermal power plant modernization trends, BHEL technology partnerships, and Make in India manufacturing opportunities. Engaged with procurement officers' posts and participated in power sector LinkedIn groups, generating 420+ connection requests from power plant personnel and PSU procurement teams.
Results: GeM platform orders increased from ₹2.4 crore (pre-campaign baseline 12 months) to ₹9.1 crore (campaign 8 months + 4-month post-campaign measurement), representing 280% growth. Website traffic from procurement decision-makers (identified through LinkedIn referrals and ".gov.in" domain visits) increased 540%, with average session duration of 4 minutes 15 seconds indicating deep content engagement. Technical blog articles generated 15 direct procurement inquiries resulting in ₹1.8 crore orders from new customers outside BHEL ecosystem. LinkedIn content established director as industry thought leader, resulting in speaking invitation at Indian Power Station Engineers Association conference and feature article in industry trade publication. Client attributes 65% of new customer acquisition to content marketing initiatives demonstrating ₹38:1 campaign ROI.

Temple's daily 12,000-18,000 visitor footfall (swelling to 150,000+ during Vaikunta Ekadasi festival) generates ₹2,800+ crore economic impact through direct temple hundi collections (₹180+ crore annually), accommodation in 850+ hotels-lodges-dharmashalas, prasadam sales from 400+ shops lining Rajagopuram approach streets, and transportation services including 1,200+ auto-rickshaws serving temple corridor. Content marketing targeting this pilgrimage economy addresses three primary segments: first-time pilgrims from interstate-international locations requiring comprehensive darshan guidance content navigating 21 gopuram temple layout and ritual protocols, local devotees seeking daily puja schedule updates and special event notifications building digital engagement habits, and temple service providers (hotels-prasadam vendors-tour operators) requiring visibility content differentiating offerings in highly competitive market.

First-time pilgrim orientation content must balance religious devotion sanctity with practical accessibility guidance addressing common frustration points: temple's 156-acre complex layout spanning seven concentric prakaram (enclosures) with 21 gopuram gateways creating navigation confusion where visitors unfamiliar with architecture waste 2-3 hours searching for sanctum sanctorum (primary deity location) rather than following prescribed circumambulation route, afternoon closure timing (11 AM-4 PM) catching unaware visitors arriving at midday forcing return visits or rushed morning darshan compromising spiritual experience quality, and dress code protocols (mandatory dhoti for men, saree-compliant attire for women in innermost prakarams) requiring either advance preparation or on-site garment rental from 80+ shops surrounding temple creating unexpected expense and time delays. Effective content addresses these challenges through visual navigation guides using annotated temple layout maps identifying key landmarks (Arjuna-facing entrance gopuram, Chandra Pushkarini sacred tank, Nammalvar shrine), optimal visit timing recommendations prioritizing 6-8 AM morning darshan for minimal crowds and full darshan access before afternoon closure, and packing checklists covering dress code requirements-prasadam containers-photography restrictions preventing common visitor mistakes.
