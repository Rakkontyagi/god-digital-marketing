Arunachal Pradesh - Land of Rising Sun: India's Easternmost Strategic Border State

Arunachal Pradesh, meaning "Land of Dawn-lit Mountains," is India's easternmost state where the sun rises first on Indian soil, approximately 1-2 hours before rest of country at 5:00-5:30 AM IST. Located in Northeast India, Arunachal Pradesh shares international borders with Bhutan 217 km western frontier, China 1,129 km McMahon Line northern-eastern border, and Myanmar 520 km southeastern frontier, making it strategically significant border state with three nations. The state capital Itanagar at 350 meters elevation serves as political-administrative center, while Tawang 3,048 meters in northwestern Himalayan reaches is spiritual-tourism capital housing India's largest Buddhist monastery. Arunachal Pradesh covers 83,743 sq km India's 16th largest state with 17 Lakh population 2021 census making it least densely populated 17 persons/sq km with vast wilderness intact.
